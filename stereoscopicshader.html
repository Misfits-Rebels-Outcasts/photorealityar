Stereoscopic Shaders

 

Ocean Wave

Ocean Wave is a real-time shader that simulates the reflective, dynamic surface of a stylized ocean environment. The scene is built using raymarching across a procedural distance field that represents layered waveforms created through fractal noise and harmonic synthesis. These waveforms are animated smoothly over time to emulate fluid motion.

Lighting is driven by multiple dynamic sources, including directional sunlight and ambient reflections. Surface normals are computed with high precision, and subtle ambient occlusion enhances visual depth. The shader uses smooth blending functions to sculpt organic wave shapes and reflections.

Color is influenced by view-dependent effects such as fresnel reflection and surface shading, with hues dynamically modulated using procedural HSV-based gradients. The final color is adjusted for visual clarity and tone, creating a clean, bright, and immersive water-like appearance. Temporal controls support beat-synced animation, and multi-sample antialiasing is used for smooth image quality.

 

 

Terrain Sculpt

The Terrain Sculpt shader creates beautiful, stylized 3D landscapes using procedural generation. It mathematically sculpts terrain using noise, patterns, and shapes. Different terrain styles like hills, spines, caverns, and slopes can be selected and blended together. The shader simulates how light interacts with the ground, including shadows, highlights, reflections, and ambient color, giving the scene depth and realism. A special "warp" effect can animate the terrain, making it ripple and flow over time. Camera position, lighting, and perspective are fully adjustable, allowing creative control over the final look. The result is a responsive, immersive terrain that feels both natural and artistic.

Red vs Blue Gyroid

Red vs Blue Gyroid is a stereoscopic ray-marched shader that visualizes a dynamic volumetric gyroid structure using layered procedural noise. The core of the shader is a signed distance function that blends multiple gyroid fields to produce complex, morphing forms. Colors transition between red and blue tones based on structural depth, lighting, and surface orientation. Real-time illumination includes directional lighting, surface reflections, and soft shadow simulation. Normals are refined with edge correction techniques to enhance visual clarity. The scene features animated movement, dynamic camera positioning, and a high-dynamic-range output with soft spatial anti-aliasing.

 

 

Apollonian Glow

Apollonian Glow is a dynamic fractal shader that visualizes a volumetric structure inspired by recursive geometric inversion and modular symmetry. The core rendering technique uses raymarching to explore a procedurally folded space, repeatedly applying mirrored transformations, rotations, and spherical inversions. This generates intricate, glowing formations reminiscent of 3D Apollonian gaskets.

Color is driven by logarithmic scaling of the geometric detail, producing smooth gradients and prismatic shifts through the fractal. An internal glow accumulates along the ray path, modulated by distance and iteration count, resulting in a luminous, organic feel. Beat-synchronized time modulation enables rhythm-aware animations, while multisample antialiasing ensures visual clarity. A soft tonal adjustment and gamma correction are applied in the final output.

 

 

Cloudy Crystal Marble Glow

Cloudy Crystal Marble Glow is a volumetric ray-marched shader that simulates the appearance of a translucent crystalline sphere with intricate internal marble patterns. The shader combines procedural fractal noise and advanced ray-object intersection techniques to create soft, glowing structures within a transparent medium. Light is scattered and refracted through a fourth-order sphere surface, with dynamic lighting and fake soft shadows enhancing depth and realism. The visual effect includes a refractive surface layer, internal glow, and subtle surface reflections, all animated over time with optional beat-synchronized timing. A post-processing pass applies tone shaping, gamma correction, and mild vignette-style falloff for added depth. Anti-aliasing is achieved via a multi-sample jittered accumulation pass.
